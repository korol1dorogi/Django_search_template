AI is increasingly deployed to tackle climate change and biodiversity loss. Machine learning models analyze satellite imagery to track deforestation, monitor glacier melt, and detect illegal fishing vessels in real time. These algorithms process massive amounts of Earth observation data far faster than human analysts. In agriculture, intelligent systems optimize irrigation and fertilizer use by predicting weather patterns and soil conditions, reducing water waste and chemical runoff. Smart grids use AI to balance renewable energy supply (solar, wind) with demand, minimizing reliance on fossil fuels. Another promising application is wildlife protection: acoustic sensors paired with deep learning can identify poachers’ gunshots or track endangered species via their calls. Climate scientists also train AI models to simulate complex atmospheric processes, improving extreme weather forecasts. However, training large AI models consumes substantial electricity, sometimes offsetting environmental benefits. Researchers are developing “green AI” techniques that prioritize energy efficiency and smaller model sizes. By combining IoT sensors, drones, and intelligent algorithms, we can build early warning systems for natural disasters. Ultimately, AI offers powerful tools for sustainability, but only if deployed responsibly and powered by clean energy.
